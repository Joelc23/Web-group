  • What is Climate Action?

    Climate action refers to efforts taken to combat climate change and its impacts. These efforts involve reducing greenhouse gas emissions (climate mitigation) and/or taking action to prepare for and adjust to both the current effects of climate change and the predicted impacts in the future (climate adaptation).

  • The Climate Action Plan 2024

    The purpose of the Climate Action Plan is to lay out a roadmap of actions which will ultimately lead us to meeting our national climate objective of pursuing and achieving, by no later than the end of the year 2050, the transition to a climate resilient, biodiversity rich, environmentally sustainable and climate neutral economy.

  • Main goal of Climate Action.

    The main goal of Climate Action is to combat climate change and its impacts. Climate change is now affecting every country on every continent. It is disrupting national economies and affecting lives, costing people, communities and countries dearly today and even more tomorrow.

  • The 3 pillars of Climate Action

    The 3 pillars of Climate Action are Mitigation, Adaptation and Means of Implementation. Adaptation are all the actions taken to manage the risks of climate change impacts, and mitigation is all the actions taken to reduce the emissions that cause climate change. Each pillar has various issues and policies that show the ways climate action affects our daily lives.
